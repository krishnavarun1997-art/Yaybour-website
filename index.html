Why we're building this

Someone nearby could have saved a life. No one knew.

A few years ago, someone needed blood urgently. A matching donor lived close enough to walk over. Neither one had any way of knowing the other existed.

That's not a rare story. It happens in every Indian city, every day — the exact help you need is usually close enough to reach, and completely invisible to you.

Yaybour exists to close that gap. Not with another feed to scroll. With a network that actually knows who's near you, and what they can do.

Before you ask

A few honest answers

Will my exact address be visible to other neighbours?+
No. Your radius is used to match you to the right groups and asks — your exact address is never shown to other users.
How is this different from a building WhatsApp group?+
WhatsApp groups are per-building and unsearchable once a message scrolls past. Yaybour organises your whole neighbourhood — not just one building — into structured, searchable groups, so asks and offers don't get lost.
Is Yaybour free to use?+
Yes — Yaybour is completely free for residents, with no paid plan. Businesses have free and paid tiers at launch.
How are businesses verified?+
Through DigiLocker, so business owner identity is confirmed through an official, government-backed channel — not just a self-declared listing.
When does Yaybour launch in my city?+
We're launching in Mumbai real soon. Other cities to follow.
